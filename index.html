Investigating Global Nutrition

Malnutrition is a Global Problem

Every country is affected by malnutrition whether directly or indirectly. The Global Nutrition Report was first conceived in 2013 to "[track] the commitments made by 100 stakeholders spanning governments, aid donors, civil society, the UN and businesses"[1]. It classified countries by the different forms of malnutrition they face and tracks their progress.




Although we traditionally think of malnutrition as undernutrition (wasting, stunting, underweight), there are actually 3 main categories of malnutrition according to the World Health Organization [2]:

  1. undernutrition: wasting, stunting, or underweight
  2. micronutrient-related malnutrition: deficiencies or excess micronutrients such as calcium
  3. overweight, obesity and diet-related noncommunicable diseases

Tracking Progress

Two types of malnutrition that the Global Nutrition Report tracks are obesity and diabetes. In the 2018 report, it identifies which countries are on track to reach pre-determined goals, and which are behind, as well as the percent of the male and female population that have these conditions.

Diving Deeper: Micronutrients

Another thing Americans often don't realize counts at malnutrition, despite the multitude of advertisements (such as the "Got Milk" posters throughout the 2000s and early 2010s found in many public schools across the country, and the various click-baiting articles about sugar consumption!) is related to micronutrient consumption and dietary needs. The Global Health Nutrition Report is one of the first efforts to estimate consumption of certain food groups, nutrients, and other dietary components that contribute to malnutrition not just in the United States and Western Europe, but all over the world.

What role does money play in malnutrition?

Under 5 mortality rates (per 1000 live births) are another indicator of malnutrition. If we look at the relationship between that and per capita GDP, we are not surprised to see a correlation: richer countries in general have lower under 5 mortality rates.
