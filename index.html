Investigating Health Determinants by Country

Malnutrition is a Global Problem

Every country is affected by malnutrition whether directly or indirectly. The Global Nutrition Report was first conceived in 2013 to "[track] the commitments made by 100 stakeholders spanning governments, aid donors, civil society, the UN and businesses"[1]. It classified countries by the different forms of malnutrition they face and tracks their progress.

Tracking Progress

Two types of malnutrition that the Global Nutrition Report tracks are obesity and diabetes. In the 2018 report, it identifies which countries are on track to reach pre-determined goals, and which are behind.
Is obesity or diabetes a regional, sub-regional, or country specific problem? Play around with the tree below to see.

Diving Deeper: Dietary Needs

In the United States, we often see news and campaigns that address specific dietary goals, such as the "Got Milk" posters throughout the 2000s and early 2010s found in many public schools across the country, and the various click-baiting articles about sugar consumption. The Global Health Nutrition Report is one of the first efforts to estimate consumption of certain food groups, nutrients, and other dietary components that contribute to malnutrition not just in the United States and Western Europe, but all over the world.
The 2018 Global Health Nutrition report estimates daily intake in grams/person of several possibly malnutrition determinants. Let's take a closer look at one of them.
